Today's Highlights

  • MS Dhoni announces retirement from international cricket

    Former India captain and two-time World Cup winner, Mahendra Singh Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket on Saturday (August 15) via an Instagram post. "Thanks a lot for ur love and support throughout.from 1929 hrs consider me as Retired (sic)", read his post of the formal announcement.The Chennai Super Kings captain, though, is expected to feature in the upcoming IPL 2020 in the UAE and was spotted earlier today arriving in Chennai for the team's brief training camp before they fly out to the middle-east.Dhoni had earlier quit Test cricket in 2014 after the Melbourne Test on the tour of Australia and had handed over ODI and T20I captaincy to Virat Kohli in January 2017.

  • PAKISTAN TOUR OF ENGLAND, 2020 England's great escape

    139 - by Jos Buttler and Chris Woakes is the highest sixth wicket partnership for England in the fourth innings of a Test, surpassing 131 by Mike Gatting and Jack Richards at the SCG in Ashes 1986/87. It is also the second highest sixth wicket stand against Pakistan in the fourth innings, behind 238 by Justin Langer and Adam Gilchrist in Hobart in 1999. 160 - Runs added by England after the fall of their fifth wicket. Only once before they have added more after losing their fifth wicket in a successful fourth innings run chase - 215 against Australia chasing 263 at the Oval in 1902.

  • James Anderson looks to next challenge: 'Can I reach 700? Why not?'

    James Anderson looks set to be a Test cricketer for at least another 18 months after revealing the England management have asked him to play on until after the next Ashes series.While there has been speculation at times this summer that Anderson, now aged 38, might consider retiring after he reached the milestone of 600 Test wickets, he insisted he has no such intentions.Instead, he has reiterated his belief he still has plenty "to offer this [England] team" and suggested the 700-wicket mark may even be within his reach.
